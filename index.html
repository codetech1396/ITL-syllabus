Overview

Introduction to Lawyering is a two-credit, year-long 1L course focusing on law school learning, the practice and business of law, professionalism, career development, and legal technology. Fall semester centers on foundational law school skills and career exploration; Spring semester emphasizes legal skills (negotiation, dispute resolution) and legal tech. Most course meetings occur during the pre-semester “compressed course week.”

Required Texts & Materials

  • JDEdge (AccessLex)
  • Roadmap: The Law Student’s Guide to Meaningful Employment (3rd Ed.)
  • Additional materials posted in Canvas (Modules)

Course Objective & Goals

The course aims to prepare students for academic success, introduce the legal profession, cultivate core lawyering skills, and develop competency with essential legal technologies.

Specific Objective Areas

  • Core law-school skill development (reading, analysis, issue spotting, advising)
  • Understanding the profession, networking, client relationships
  • Negotiation & dispute resolution fundamentals
  • Using legal technology for writing, research, analysis, and practice

Learning Outcomes

Students will understand law school expectations, professional identity formation, and foundational skills. They demonstrate learning through class participation, written assignments, simulations, and a final exam.

Evaluation & Credit (Pass/Fail)

  • May not miss more than one class meeting per semester
  • Must complete all written assignments successfully
  • Must be consistently prepared and participate in good faith
  • Must earn a satisfactory score on the Spring final exam

A small portion of students may receive an S+ for exceptional performance.

Written Assignments

  • Resume (submit in Canvas + email to assigned CPD Advisor)
  • Cover letter
  • Career development statement (~2 pages)

All assignments are due at the beginning of class on the assigned day. Instructions appear on Canvas under “Assignments.”

Course Policies

Attendance

Mandatory under ABA and Law School rules. One absence per semester permitted. Religious holiday absences excused with prior notice. Emergencies and illness go through Student Life. Arriving more than 10 minutes late may count as an absence.

Class Participation

Students must prepare by completing readings and pre-class work, and must actively participate in discussions and exercises.

Written Assignments

All must be completed successfully to receive credit.

Classroom Behavior

Students must follow the Student Code of Conduct and Law Honor Code. Unprofessional or disruptive behavior is prohibited. Laptops and electronic devices may only be used when permitted.

Technology & AI Policy

Laptops required. AI tools may NOT be used for any coursework unless explicitly permitted by faculty. Violations are Honor Code violations.

Accommodations

Students must register with the Office of Disability Resources and provide documentation to Law Student Affairs.

Discourse & Ethos

Students should: speak thoughtfully and respectfully, listen carefully, engage rigorously, examine assumptions, and maintain civility in discussion.
